# Why I Monitor Things Before They Break

Most homeowners wait for something to fail before paying attention to it.

A sump pump gets noticed when the basement floods.

A freezer gets noticed when the food is ruined.

A well pump gets noticed when there is no water.

The Tug's Garage philosophy is different.

I believe monitoring should happen before failure.

The purpose of monitoring is not automation.

The purpose is awareness.

Every piece of equipment leaves clues before it fails.

Temperature changes.

Power consumption changes.

Run times change.

Cycles change.

Patterns change.

If we learn those patterns, we can often identify problems before they become emergencies.

## Awareness Before Automation

Many smart home systems focus on automation.

At Tug's Garage, awareness comes first.

Before I automate anything, I want to understand how it behaves.

Monitoring creates understanding.

Understanding creates reliability.

Reliability creates confidence.

## The Tug's Garage Question

When evaluating any monitoring project, I ask one simple question:

> What useful information will I know tomorrow that I didn't know yesterday?

If the answer improves awareness, the project is probably worth building.
